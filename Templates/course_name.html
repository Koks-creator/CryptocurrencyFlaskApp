What is Bitcoin? When was Bitcoin created?


Bitcoin (BTC for short) is the oldest crypto in the world. It was created in 2008 by a person or group of people hiding under the code name Satoshi Nakamoto. This coin differs from the traditional currencies you know so far, such as the Polish zloty or the US dollar in that it does not appear in physical form.

Bitcoin, like all other cryptovalutes, was placed on a so-called blockchain. In other words, it is located in a decentralized, distributed network. This means that it cannot be managed by a single unit or institution. Indeed, the power over this virtual coin is exercised by all the users of its network.

Mining (digging) Bitcoin - what is it?

As you probably guess, Bitcoins do not come from the air. They are extracted by means of special devices called cryptovalutowtmi excavators. This whole process is called mining or simply digging BTC. The people who do this are called cryptocurrency miners, sometimes also minerals

People who have never been in contact with this industry before and have had a completely different idea of a cryptovalut mine may be completely surprised. Why? Well, a cryptocurrency mine can actually be any room where mining equipment works on solving complicated mathematical puzzles. All this is to be rewarded with a BTC block. The initial image of a real miner with a crowbar in his hand should be put aside.

How does the Bitcoin digging process work properly and what do cryptocurrency excavators look like? Well, miners receive the so-called Proof of Work (PoW). This Proof of Work is the BTC coins. To do this work, however, you need devices with high computing power.

What is Ethereum? Basic information


Probably when you hear about Ethereum you can imagine cryptographers like Bitcoin (BTC) or Ripple. It is important for you to understand at the very beginning that his role goes much further. In fact, it is an open source platform based on blockchain technology. It allows developers to create and implement decentralized applications.

The functioning of Ethereum can be compared to the Internet in a way. Nowadays, huge amounts of data are in the clouds or in the hands of technological giants such as Google and social giants like Facebook. These companies need many specialists who make sure that all information about users is secure. Unfortunately, there is a very high risk of hacking attacks. The tactics chosen by cybercriminals are becoming increasingly sophisticated. As a result, there is a danger that someone without our consent and knowledge will be able to steal or modify data belonging to us.

Why are we talking about all this? Well, this gap can be filled by the Ethereum. Its main goal is to eliminate all third parties that have so far been responsible for storing information. All this is possible thanks to blockchain technology. Ethereum allows applications to operate in a distributed system. This in turn means that data is not stored on individual servers, but is distributed among a huge number of computers from around the world. All information is transparent, its destruction or modification is not so easy.

Data stored on one server can be lost or changed very quickly. If they are on multiple computers, one user will not be able to destroy them. The only way would be a 51% attack. This means that more than half of the people in the network would have to collide. With millions of servers from around the world this is almost impossible.

The vision behind the creation of Ethereum was to create the so-called computer of the world, i.e. a huge network supporting various applications, without the intermediation of third parties. An important advantage of Ethereum, which should be emphasized, is that unlike other blockchain computers, it is programmable. What does this mean? Well, it can be used to create new applications: building cryptographic portfolios, financial platforms, games and many other things.

What is hard fork?


Before we move on to discussing what Bitcoin Cash (BCH) actually is, we need to explain the term: hard fork. Understanding this is very important. This will give you an overview of how Bitcoin's younger brother (BTC) was born. You will also find out what purpose it was created for. So let's get started!

Hard fork is nothing but a branch of a blockchain (chain of blocks) of a given cryptovalute into two separate parts. Both of them share the same history/future, but face a completely different future. During hard fork, some modifications/upgrades are added to one of the blockchain pages. This is a change in the software that is responsible for the functioning of the cryptovalute.

What is the purpose of hard forks, who decides about them and whether they are necessary? Due to the fact that one of the two parts of the blockchain is changed in the protocol, according to the (new) rules it prevents outdated nodes from accepting blocks generated by the latest software version. The nodes that do not pass the update process will take a completely different direction from the updated ones, not according to the developers' assumptions.

Hard fork Bitcoin. The history of Bitcoin Cash


As you can guess, there was a lot of discussion about hard fork Bitcoin. It's worth adding that for most programmers, code in cryptography is sacred and should not be changed. Nevertheless, with more and more users coming to the cryptovalut market, Bitcoin itself began to struggle with an extremely important problem. It was unable to process so many transactions in its own network.

Due to the fact that each new BTC block is generated every 10 minutes, and its size is only 1 MB, numerous controversial issues have arisen at this level. While the Bitcoin miners demanded an increase in the size of BTC blocks, the programmers were against it. They presented a completely different solution to this problem. In their opinion, implementing Segregates Witness (SegWit) would be sufficient to increase the amount of data in each block. Another problem was that Bitcoin began to be seen not as a means of payment but as an investment asset.

Both sides unfortunately did not manage to come to an agreement, so on August 1, 2017 the hard fork on Bitcoin was reached. This resulted in two blockchins: Bitcoin (BTC) and Bitcoin Cash (BTC). It is worth noting that they differ in principle only in the size of the blocks. Of course, this improvement allows to make more transactions with BCH. However, Bitcoin Cash still uses the same algorithm as Bitcoin

Bitcoin (BTC) and Bitcoin Cash (BCH) - differences


Although many people believe that Bitcoin Cash will never be able to replace the real Bitcoin, in many respects it has taken the lead over it. One should take into account, for example, the fact that the BCH network is more scalable than BTC. This allows transactions to be made in seconds, not minutes. If we are on the subject, it should be added that the transaction fees are also much lower.

However, since Bitcoin is well established on the market, it is the most popular and best known cryptic, in some respects these features give it a forum. In the case of mining (cryptovalent mining), miners find it more profitable to dig BTC than BCH. Where does this come from? Well, the cost of extracting both of these cryptovalutes is relatively similar, but the price for one Bitcoin is much higher. Therefore, a miner extracting this cryptocurrency will earn more than a miner focusing on digging for Bitcoin Cash.

Bitcoin also enjoys greater trust among investors. As we have already mentioned, it is a leading crypto player. BCH is losing to BTC in terms of popularity, which is why it is listed on fewer crypto exchanges. Due to the fact that he also has fewer currency pairs on different platforms, he becomes less marketable than his older brother. Also, the number of crypto wallets dedicated to Bitcoin Cash is much smaller.

One of the main features of BCH is certainly safety. It is based on Bitcoin Core blockchain, and the community describes it as one of the most resistant blockchain technologies. However, Bitcoin has much more community support behind it, making it more decentralized. Moreover, unlike BCH, BTC is in the hands of its users.

What is Chainlink? For what purpose was it launched?


This project was created to solve the problem that smart contracts face. Well, they did not have access to data on their own. When the programmers were implementing the chosen smart contract, they encountered a communication problem. It was unable to connect to key external resources such as offline data or APIs (a strict set of rules and descriptions of how computer programs communicate with each other).

Lack of external connectivity was a problem for every smart network of contracts. What's more, connecting smart contracts with input data via a single node was not a good solution, as this was the point of failure. The solution to this problem was the highly decentralized Oracle Chainlink network.

It's worth knowing: Chainlink provides the same security guarantee as the intelligent contracts themselves.

Key information: The White Paper of the Chainlink project was published on September 4, 2017. This network provides an easy way to combine intelligent contracts with the data sources and APIs that are necessary for their operation. Chainlink also allows to transfer funds. This project cooperates with leaders of banking technology such as SWIFT. In this way it connects banks with intelligent contracts.

The motto of the Chainlink project is that intelligent contracts should be reliable! This means that the input and output on which such a contract is based must be secure!

Who created Chailnik? The history of project creation


This project was founded by Sergei Nazarov, currently CEO of Chainlink, and Steve Ellis, Director of Technology. The company behind it is called SmartContract. It is based in San Francisco.

In September 2017 Chainlink collected 32 million dollars in the initial coinage offer (ICO). A billion LINK tokens were issued, the use of which will be described in more detail in the following chapters.

In November 2018 Chainlink took over the Town Crier system. This is a highly reputable smart contract and a project focused on data protection. This system complements the Oracle Chainlink system.

What problems does Town Crier solve? Well, it uses Intel SGX technology to provide a strong guarantee that data comes from an existing, trustworthy source.

It's worth knowing: The innovative Intel SGX technology is becoming increasingly popular among many applications designed to protect programs from outside interference. This abbreviation fully stands for Software Guard Extensions. It defines private memory regions of the computer, called enclaves.

Even the operators of the Town Crier server are not able to manipulate the data processed by this system. And what use does it gain in the Chainlink project? Well, it increases the privacy and verifiable authenticity of the data that enters the decentralized Oracle network.

IMPORTANT: By using Town Crier, Oracle's Chainlink nodes will be able to ensure that the data they provide has not been altered BEFORE or BEFORE being transferred to an intelligent agreement.

In May 2019 Chainlink was launched in the Ethereum network.

What is Cardano? Who created this project?


Cardano is a cryptovalut project evolving over time. The idea of its creation was born in 2015 - interestingly, it did not have an authoritative white paper. Its aim was and is to go beyond the schemes. It aims to change the way cryptovalut is designed and developed. Cardano wants to provide a more diverse and thus sustainable ecosystem, taking into account the dynamically changing needs of users as well as systems that strive for integration.

This is an Open Source project - with an open-source code. The Cardano team assumes that it is the ability to make a soft or hard fork that is critical to the long-term success of a cryptovert.

This project is headed by Charles Hoskinson. Moreover, he is also co-founder of Ethereum. He started his collaboration with Vitalik Buterin in late 2013. He left this project in June 2014. Before he became interested in blockchain, he studied analytical number theory. A colleague persuaded him to create a new project called Input-Output Hong Kong (IOHK). This is how the engineering and research company was established, focused on the construction of cryptovalut and blockchain.

Cardano was not launched until 2017, when it raised $60 million in public collections (ICO). Interestingly, it is one of the first platforms to be built in the Haskell programming language. The team behind this project consists of researchers and engineers with a lot of knowledge and experience. Cardano is supported by three organizations with separate functions in terms of ownership and leadership, these are: The IOHK, which designs and builds this project, Emurgo, which is responsible for supporting commercial projects, and the Cardano Foundation, which oversees the development of the ecosystem.

What is an ADA crypted? What is its function?


ADA is a cryptocurrency belonging to the Cardano project. Users from all over the world can use it for safe and fast exchange of values, without the need to involve a third party in the process. Each such transaction is recorded on the Cardano blockchain.

Is there a Bitcoin SV (BSV)?


Bitcoin SV (BSV) is a cryptocurrency, which was created by the hard fork of Bitcoin Cash (BCH). When the network in Bitcoin (BTC) itself was broken up, the newly created crypto community did not fully agree on its further development. As a result, two camps were formed around Bitcoin Cash. One of them remained under the assumptions that BCH had adopted from the very beginning, while the other one aimed to give the miners another choice of consensus rules for Bitcoin Cash. Therefore, on November 15, 2018, the network was broken up and Bitcoin SV, also known as Bitcoin Cash SV (BCHSV), was formed.

Who created Bitcoin SV and for what purpose?

The cryptovaluta Bitcoin SV is developed by a company called nChain, headed by Craig Wright. You probably have heard that name more than once. All because this man claims to be the creator of Bitcoin - Satoshim Nakamoto.

Craig Wright has repeatedly tried to convince the world of his thesis. However, he never provided enough evidence to support it. As a result, the crypto community hailed him as the self-proclaimed Satoshim Nakamoto.

What was the purpose of Bitcoin SV? Well, as Craig Wright says, it is the realization of the vision of the Bitcoin creator himself. The full development of the abbreviation of this controversial cryptovaluta is: Bitcoin Satoshi Vishion, or the vision of Satoshi Nakamoto.

Bitcoin SV and Bitcoin Cash - differences


Although BSV shares several common features with BCH, there are a number of differences between the two cryptovalots. The basic one is the maximum capacity of the transaction block. In Bitcoin SV it is 128 MB (to be increased to 512 MB in the future), in Bitcoin Cash it is only 32 MB (initially 8 MB).

While BCH decided to introduce a canonical arrangement of transactions, BSV remained with the current topological model. Although the solution introduced by Bitcoin Cash provides much higher scalability within a block of the same capacity, it was not implemented in Bitcoin SV due to the potential risk of lowering security.

Another important difference between BCH and BSV is that the first cryptocurrency has an operating code called OP_CHECKDATASIG. It was designed to confirm messages from outside the blockchain. This is related to the development of functionality for smart contracts. Bitcoin SV, on the other hand, stated that it does not need such a solution.

What is Monero (XMR)?


Monero (XMR) is a cryptocurrency oriented towards confidential and private transactions. This virtual coin differs from Bitcoin (BTC) or Ethera (ETH) in that its blockchain is not public. No one can trace the transaction history on it, or gain access to public addresses, as is the case with most other crypts. Operations carried out through Monero become undetectable with advanced cryptography solutions.

What are the functions of this cryptocurrencies? As we have already mentioned above, it is peer-to-peer (P2P) electronic cash that allows for fast, cheap and confidential transfers of funds from one person to another without any time or border barriers.

Monero and Bitcoin - basic differences


The XMR cryptocurrency differs from BTC in many ways. Although Bitcoin is usually attributed with anonymity, in reality it has nothing to do with it. The BTC blockchain is public. This means that every user can see when, from which public address, where and how much money has been transferred. Due to the fact that most cryptographic platforms have to follow KYC and AML policies, it is easy to get to the blockchain. In other words, if there is verification on the stock exchange, the company knows who the public address belongs to and, if necessary, is obliged to provide information to the relevant law enforcement authorities.

This is completely different for Monero. Although this cryptovlaut is not as popular as BTC, it gains an advantage in terms of transaction privacy. Of course, this is not the only difference between the two coins.

What is Stellar?


Stellar is a decentralized open-source payment protocol. It allows for fast international transactions using any currency pair. This project has its own blockchain.

Transactions made in the Stellar network are added to a common, distributed database, accessible to every user around the world. Interestingly, Stellar uses its own unique consensus method. It allows for quick yet cheap transactions in just a few seconds.

What was the purpose of Stellar?


The exchange of fiduciary, crypto or token currencies can be a long and costly process. Stellar solves this problem by becoming a global network that is able to handle thousands of such exchanges per second. This project offers solutions to fill the gaps between traditional financial organizations and users.

Stellar provides tools that allow for fast and cheap cross-border transfers. Currently, making such transfers through banks becomes not only time-consuming but also expensive. This project therefore offers almost zero conversion, cooperating with several payment processors as well as banking institutions.

In other words, Stellar works with centralized organizations in a decentralized way. It solves the problems of traditional payment systems.

What is Stellar crypted (XML)?


The Stellar network has its own native XML crypto, also called Lumen. It has a function similar to Ethera in Ethereum. You could say that XML powers the Stellar network. This crypto is used, among other things, to pay trading fees and maintain accounts on the Stellar network.

The project assumes that each account for XML has to have a small amount of this crypted. This is due to the basic construction of the Stellar book. Without a nominal barrier it could be filled with spam. This in turn would be an obstacle in becoming a fast and efficient payment system. Currently the minimum balance is 1 XML. The minimum transaction fee is 0.00001 XML.

Initially, 100 billion XML tokens were issued. In November 2019, the overall supply was halved. So there are only 50 billion Lumen coins and there will be no more. While 20 billion XML tokens were released, the remaining 30 billion were retained by the Stellar Development Foundation for further development and promotion.
